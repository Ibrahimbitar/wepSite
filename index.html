Apple

Apple Inc. is a tech company based in Cupertino, California. Founded in 1976 by Steve Jobs, Steve Wozniak, and Ronald Wayne, the company designs, develops, and sells consumer electronics, computer software, and online services. Apple is best known for its hardware products, including the iPhone smartphone, iPad tablet, Mac personal computer, and Apple Watch smartwatch. It also sells digital content through its iTunes Store, iCloud storage service, and Apple Music streaming service..

Samsung

The Samsung Galaxy A3 is a mid-range Android smartphone produced by the South Korean electronics giant. It was released in December 2014 and features a 4.5-inch Super AMOLED display, quad-core 1.2 GHz processor, 1 GB of RAM and 16 GB internal storage. The device also sports a 5 MP rear-facing camera and a 1900 mAh battery. It runs on the Android 4.4 KitKat operating system and is available in black, white, gold and pink.

Microsoft

Microsoft is an American multinational technology company that develops, manufactures, licenses, and sells computer software, consumer electronics, personal computers, and related services. It is best known for its Windows operating system, the Microsoft Office suite of productivity software, Xbox gaming consoles, and Surface lineup of touchscreen laptops and tablets. It has a wide range of products and services, including cloud-based solutions, artificial intelligence, and productivity tools. Microsoft was founded by Bill Gates and Paul Allen in 1975. The company headquarters are located in Redmond, Washington, USA.

twitar

Twitar is an online social media platform developed by Twi technologies. It is similar to other popular social media platforms like Twitter and Facebook. Users can create a profile and post content that their friends and followers can see, in the form of posts, photos, and other content. Users can also interact with each other through comments, direct messages, and likes. Twitar also has a feature called "twits", which are short posts that can be updated quickly. Twitar also offers online events, groups, and games.

Alphabet

Alphabet offers services and products related to internet search, cloud computing, online advertising technologies, software, hardware, and artificial intelligence. Its core subsidiaries include Google, Calico, DeepMind, GV, CapitalG, Verily, Waymo, X, and Loon. Other subsidiaries include Google Fiber, Nest Labs, and Google Research.
